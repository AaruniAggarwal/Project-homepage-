How to keep liver healthy – 4 Ways to Keep Your Liver Healthy
The liver is one of the most important organs in the human body. It performs more than 500 functions and plays an important role in maintaining the overall functioning of the body. The primary function of the liver is to process the food you eat. It is therefore vital that you keep your liver healthy and ensure that it is functioning efficiently. Because, without it you cannot survive. Here a few important tips to maintain your liver health. Limit your alcohol consumption: There are many students that suggest that consuming alcohol in moderate quantity is beneficial for health. However, alcohol abuse can be detrimental and if left uncontrolled it can be fatal. The human liver has the capacity to only process a small amount of alcohol every hour. Beyond that it can damage the liver, which over a period can lead to fat build-up in the liver, swelling or inflammation. If you are already suffering from a liver diseases, even a small quality of alcohol can worsen your condition.To keep your liver healthy, it is advisable that limit your alcohol consumption. Several health experts have corroborated that men must limit their alcohol intake to two drinks in a day, whereas the limit for women is one drink per day. Controlling your alcohol intake would keep your liver healthy for long.
Drink plenty of water: If you are looking for tips on how to improve liver health, one of the easiest things to do is drink plenty of water through the day. Your body needs to remain sufficiently hydrated. Failing to drink water will lead to toxin build up in the body, which in turn will affect the efficient functioning of the kidney, and the liver.Studies suggest that dehydration affects the liver’s ability to carry out the detoxification process, which in lead to fat build-up in the liver. It is therefore important that you drink at least 3-4 litres of water every day.
Maintain a healthy body weight: This is another effective way to not just keep your liver healthy but maintain your overall well-being. Being over overweight or obese is one of the major causes of developing liver diseases. You can maintain a healthy body weight and keep your liver healthy through a combination of regular exercise and a balanced diet.
Say NO to fatty food and eat fibre-rich food: One of the most common causes of fatty liver diseases is the presence of high levels and cholesterol and fat in the blood. You can reduce the cholesterol build up in the body by reducing the intake of the saturated fats by cutting down on eating red meats, fried food, packaged food items, canned soda. The liver stores the excess dietary fat and it lead to liver diseases because of the fat build up. Doctors recommend eating food items that are rich in fibre contents like fruits, whole grains and vegetables. Also, many nutritionists suggest eating more white meat, fish, and nuts than red meat for proteins. Eating a healthy diet that is low on fat and rich in fibre content is a great way to maintain liver health.
